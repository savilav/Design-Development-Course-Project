# Cybersecurity Awareness for University Students

### Project Overview
This project raises awareness about the cybersecurity risks faced by university students, such as phishing messages, unsafe campus Wi-Fi, and reused passwords across platforms. It provides students with practical strategies to protect their personal and academic data while promoting safer digital habits across the campus community.

## **Understanding Daily Online Risks for Students**
Students frequently browse grades, submit assignments, join class meetings, and stream content across multiple devices. Many use public or campus Wi-Fi without realizing how easily unprotected data can be exposed.

## **Why Cybersecurity Matters in Universities**
Universities are major targets for cyberattacks due to large volumes of stored personal data and academic research. Real incidentsâ€”such as the 2025 University of Pennsylvania breachâ€”show how attackers impersonate official accounts and access sensitive student information.

## **Common Cyber Threats Facing Students**
- Phishing text messages and fake university alerts  
- Scholarship scams and financial-aid fraud  
- Unsafe public Wi-Fi connections  
- Password reuse across Canvas, email, and banking  

## **Protection Techniques**
Students can reduce their risk by:
- Using strong, unique passwords  
- Enabling multi-factor authentication  
- Using VPNs on public Wi-Fi  
- Keeping software and devices updated  
- Avoiding suspicious links or attachments

## **Campus Resources**
Universities provide help desks, cybersecurity teams, training workshops, and password-reset support. National organizations like CISA and Google Safety Center also offer student-friendly tools and safety checkups.

## **Encouraging Cyber-Safe Behavior**
Any student can join cybersecurity clubs, attend awareness events, or take free online cybersecurity courses. Promoting these habits strengthens the entire campus community.
